# Peripheral Attention Trap

## Discovery Statement

In anti-money laundering transaction monitoring, a recurring failure mode emerges when compliance systems concentrate investigative resources on high-centrality nodes within a transaction graph while structurally identical laundering patterns operate through peripheral nodes that exhibit low individual connectivity but high aggregate throughput. The peripheral attention trap describes the systematic under-detection of layering schemes that exploit the cognitive and algorithmic bias toward central hubs, allowing illicit funds to traverse low-salience pathways that converge at sink accounts without triggering standard alert thresholds.

## Intuition

Standard AML graph analytics prioritize nodes with high degree centrality, betweenness centrality, or PageRank scores. Sophisticated laundering networks exploit the inverse: distributing transaction volume across many peripheral nodes, each appearing benign in isolation. The convergence pattern at sink accounts remains invisible until aggregate peripheral flow is reconstructed. The trap operates because attention allocation—both human and algorithmic—follows a power-law distribution that mirrors social network topology rather than adversarial transaction topology.
