# Peripheral Attention Trap in AML Monitoring

## Discovery Statement

AML monitoring systems that allocate primary analytical focus to high-priority transaction corridors create systematic blind zones in peripheral data streams. Fraud actors exploit the predictable distribution of analyst attention by embedding suspicious activity patterns within low-priority peripheral channels, where monitoring thresholds remain elevated and review latency extends beyond actionable windows. The emergent pattern manifests as a measurable drift in detection rates correlating inversely with channel priority rankings.

## Intuition

The discovery draws from the concept of **attention allocation asymmetry** in cognitive systems. When AML platforms implement tiered alert prioritization, the monitoring architecture implicitly defines zones of reduced scrutiny. Sophisticated actors reverse-engineer these priority hierarchies through iterative probing, identifying peripheral channels where anomalous patterns persist below detection thresholds. The trap operates as a self-reinforcing cycle: reduced monitoring investment in peripheral channels yields fewer alerts, which further justifies deprioritization, expanding the exploitable surface area.
