# Peripheral Attention Trap

## Discovery Statement

In AML transaction monitoring, a systematic blind spot emerges when compliance systems concentrate investigative attention on high-centrality network nodes while peripheral nodes—entities with low degree but high-value sink behavior—accumulate illicit funds through structurally dispersed pathways. The peripheral attention trap describes the pattern where monitoring heuristics, optimized for hub-and-spoke typologies, fail to detect value convergence at graph edges, allowing layering operations to complete undetected before any alert threshold triggers.

## Intuition

Standard AML graph analytics prioritize nodes with high betweenness centrality or degree centrality as suspicious. However, sophisticated launderers exploit peripheral positioning: funds flow through many low-degree intermediaries, each appearing benign in isolation, before converging at a final sink node. The convergence event itself may appear as a single large transaction from a previously quiet entity, but the upstream dispersal pattern—distributed across dozens of peripheral edges—never triggers attention. The trap operates because monitoring systems evaluate nodes independently rather than tracking the collective flow topology toward peripheral sinks.
