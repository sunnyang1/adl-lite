# Peripheral Attention Trap

## Discovery Statement

In AML transaction monitoring, a peripheral attention trap emerges when compliance resources concentrate on high-centrality nodes within a transaction graph, causing illicit fund flows that route through low-centrality peripheral nodes to evade detection. The trap is characterized by sink convergence patterns where multiple peripheral nodes channel funds toward a single aggregation point, exploiting the monitoring system's inherent bias toward central network positions. Detection effectiveness degrades significantly when the ratio of peripheral-to-central node monitoring falls below a critical threshold, creating blind spots that sophisticated launderers exploit systematically.

## Intuition

The peripheral attention trap draws from the concept of attention allocation in neural network architectures, where peripheral features receive disproportionately low weight during inference. In AML graph analysis, the same phenomenon manifests when risk scoring algorithms assign lower suspicion scores to nodes with few direct connections, even when peripheral nodes participate in structurally suspicious sink convergence patterns. The trap becomes particularly dangerous when peripheral nodes form hidden chains that connect to legitimate business accounts, masking the true flow of illicit funds behind a facade of normal commercial activity.
